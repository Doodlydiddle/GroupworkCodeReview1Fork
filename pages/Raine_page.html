Robots in Medicine

In 2020, the CMR Surgical's Versius robot was used by NHS hospitals for the first time.

The Western General Hospital in Edinburgh and the Milton Keynes University were the first to use it.

Versius is designed to help with minimal access procedures which is done with small incisions to the body

Versius may be able to help carry out 700 keyhole (aka laparoscopic procedures) surgeries every year.

Advantages of keyhole surgery over open surgery include:

  • Shorter and less painful recovery
  • Lower infection risk
  • Less pain and bleeding after operation

The robots were used in colorectal surgeries to help those with bowel disease and bowel cancer

The system is small enough that it can be transported easily, between different theatres and between hospitals

The system takes 15 minutes to set up so it is not very time consuming to use & will reduce the time patients spend in recovery
